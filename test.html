Saudi Arabian Oil Company (Saudi Aramco)

Jazan, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

January 2021 - September 2021

The purpose of this project is to provide Fire Protection System for Terminal Sulfur and Stacker Reclaim Building (TSSRB) and Belt Conveyor area in the Sulfur Forming Unit of Marine Terminal Facilities in Jizan.

Abqaiq Plant Restoration (Job No. 3270)

Saudi Arabian Oil Company (Saudi Aramco)

Abqaiq, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

November 2019 - July 2021

The Abqaiq oil processing facility, located approximately 60km south-west of Dhahran in Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province, is the world’s biggest oil processing and crude stabilization plant.

Owned and operated by state-owned Saudi Aramco, the Abqaiq facility has a capacity to process up to seven million barrels of crude oil a day, which accounts for more than half of Saudi Arabia’s crude production capacity and approximately seven percent of the global oil supply.

Given its strategic significance, the Abqaiq facility has been prone to terrorist attacks for long, while the recent aerial strike in September 2019 caused severe damage to the facility and disrupted Saudi Arabia’s global crude supply. The objective of this project is to carry out the work inside and outside the plant associated with the restoration work such as the damaged storage tanks and refinery towers.

ARCC scope of work includes the following:
Demolition Works
Excavation Works
Backfilling Works
Civil Works
Scaffolding Works
Structural steel supports fabrication and installation
Mechanical Works
Piping Works
Tie-in Works
Hydrostatic Testing
Electrical and Instrumentation Works

Installation of New Seal Leg at DR Module C (Job No. 3570)

SABIC (HADEED)

Al Jubail, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

September 2020 - April 2022

Saudi Iron and Steel Company (HADEED) is the first iron and steel complex in Saudi Arabia. The DR “Direct Reduction” plant has a capacity of 1.75 million t/yr. Using natural gas and dressed iron ore, DR plants produce metallized iron, which can then be further processed in the steel plant.

HADEED has decided to replace the existing seal leg system with new seal leg as per recommendation by the licensor in order to maintain safe and continuous operation of DR Module C Plant because the existing seal leg pipe bundle has stuck inside the well and no maintenance activities could be carried out resulting to chocking of pipes, thus affecting plant efficiency.

The project scope includes revised basic design and engineering, supply, installation, testing and commissioning of New Seal Leg System along with the associated civil, mechanical, electrical, instrumentation and cathodic protection works.

ARCC scope of work includes the following:
Revision of Basic Design and Engineering (by Licensor "MIDREX")
Procurement of materials
Civil Works
Mechanical Works
Piping Works
Electrical Works
Instrumentation Works
